# CapCut Music Guide

## Is Music Required?

Music is not required by the assignment. The assignment requires subtitle or voiceover at minimum. However, quiet background music can make the video feel more complete.

## Recommended Style

Use instrumental music only:

- mood: bright, technology, campus, inspiring, clean
- avoid: vocals, sad piano, intense EDM, copyrighted pop songs
- length: at least 60 seconds, or loop cleanly

## Safe Sources

Recommended:

1. YouTube Audio Library
   - URL: https://www.youtube.com/audiolibrary
   - Choose tracks with "Attribution not required" if possible.
   - If attribution is required, copy the attribution text into `outputs/asset_sources.md`.

2. Pixabay Music
   - URL: https://pixabay.com/music/
   - Check the Pixabay Content License before use.
   - Still record the music page URL in `outputs/asset_sources.md`.

3. CapCut built-in audio
   - Convenient, but check whether the selected track can be used outside CapCut/TikTok and in a public GitHub submission.
   - If unsure, use YouTube Audio Library or Pixabay instead.

## CapCut Steps

1. Download one instrumental MP3.
2. In CapCut, click `Audio` or `Music`.
3. Upload or add the MP3.
4. Drag the music track under the whole 60-second video.
5. Trim music to match the video length.
6. Set volume low:
   - if there is no voiceover: around 10-18%
   - if there is voiceover: around 5-10%
7. Add fade in: 0.5-1 second.
8. Add fade out: 1-2 seconds.
9. Play the whole video once and confirm subtitles remain the main focus.

## Asset Source Entry Template

Add this to `outputs/asset_sources.md` after choosing a track:

```markdown
| background_music | music | MUSIC_PAGE_URL | LICENSE / attribution status | Quiet background music for final video |
```
